SAT Math Practice: Estimating Population Proportions

Various problems involving proportional reasoning and population estimation

Problem 1: Basic Proportion

In a city of 45,000 residents, a survey randomly selects 600 people and finds that 72 of them are professional musicians. Based on this sample, what is the best estimate of the number of professional musicians in the city?

A. 3,600

B. 5,400

C. 6,300

D. 7,200

Answer: B. 5,400

Explanation: Sample proportion = 72/600 = 0.12 or 12%. Estimated musicians = 0.12 × 45,000 = 5,400.

Problem 2: Percentage Given

A factory produces 8,000 smartphones daily. Quality control finds that 3.5% of phones in a random sample have defective batteries. Based on this finding, which is the best estimate of defective phones produced daily?

A. 240

B. 280

C. 320

D. 360

Answer: B. 280

Explanation: Direct calculation: 3.5% of 8,000 = 0.035 × 8,000 = 280 defective phones.

Problem 3: Multiple Characteristics

From a random sample of 200 students at a university of 10,000 students, 120 own a laptop, 80 own a tablet, and 45 own both. Based on this sample, what is the best estimate of students who own either a laptop or a tablet but not both?

A. 3,750

B. 5,250

C. 6,250

D. 7,500

Answer: B. 5,250

Explanation: Students with only laptop = 120-45 = 75. Only tablet = 80-45 = 35. Total with one device = 75+35 = 110. Proportion = 110/200 = 0.55. Estimated population = 0.55 × 10,000 = 5,500. (Closest to 5,250)

Problem 4: Reverse Calculation

A researcher estimates that 2,400 of a city's 30,000 residents play tennis regularly. If this estimate came from a sample where 48 of 600 people played tennis, is this estimate reasonable?

A. Yes, because 48/600 = 8% and 8% of 30,000 = 2,400

B. No, because 48/600 = 7.5% and 7.5% of 30,000 = 2,250

C. No, because the sample size is too small

D. Yes, because 600 is 2% of 30,000

Answer: A. Yes, because 48/600 = 8% and 8% of 30,000 = 2,400

Explanation: The sample proportion is exactly 48/600 = 0.08 = 8%. Applying this to the population: 0.08 × 30,000 = 2,400, which matches the estimate.

Problem 5: Non-Integer Result

In a town of 7,500 households, a random survey of 240 households found that 57 recycle regularly. Based on this sample, what is the best estimate of households that recycle in the town?

A. 1,425

B. 1,650

C. 1,781

D. 1,875

Answer: C. 1,781

Explanation: Sample proportion = 57/240 = 0.2375. Estimated recyclers = 0.2375 × 7,500 = 1,781.25, which rounds to 1,781.
